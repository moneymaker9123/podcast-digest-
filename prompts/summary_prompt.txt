You are summarizing a podcast episode for a knowledgeable professional who follows business, finance, tech/AI, and health/fitness content. The goal is to give them a complete mental model of the episode — enough to feel like they "got it" without needing to listen — while staying concise and skipping filler.

Podcast: {podcast_name}
Episode Title: {episode_title}
Guest (if applicable): {guest_name}
Transcript/Show Notes:
{content}

---

Write a structured summary using the following sections:

**🎯 The Big Idea** (2-3 sentences)
The core thesis or central argument of the episode. What is the main thing the host/guest is trying to convey?

**👤 Guest Context** (1-2 sentences, skip if no guest)
Who the guest is and *why their perspective matters* on this topic. Focus on what makes them credible or uniquely positioned, not a generic bio.

**📌 Key Points**
A numbered list of the 4-7 most substantive ideas discussed. Each point should be 2-4 sentences — enough to understand the idea, the reasoning behind it, and any implications. Prioritize depth over breadth; skip surface-level observations.

**📊 Data Points & Specifics**
Bullet list of any concrete numbers, studies, statistics, timelines, company names, or specific claims made. If none are present, omit this section.

**⚡ Contrarian or Surprising Takes**
Any claims that push back on conventional wisdom, predictions that seem bold, or perspectives that would surprise most people in this space. If nothing qualifies, omit this section.

**✅ Actionable Takeaways**
2-5 specific things a listener could actually do, research, or change based on this episode. Make these concrete, not generic ("read more about X" is weak; "look into Y product/study/framework for Z reason" is strong).

**🔖 Worth Remembering** (1 sentence)
The single most memorable or quotable insight from the episode.

---

Tone guidelines:
- Write like a smart colleague giving a briefing, not a book report
- Use plain language; avoid buzzwords unless they were specifically used and matter
- If the episode is light on substance, say so honestly rather than padding the summary
- Flag anything that seems speculative or unverified with "(unverified)" or "(guest's opinion)"
